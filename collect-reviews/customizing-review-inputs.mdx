## Collect Exactly the Feedback You Need

Customize every field in your review collection forms to match your brand voice and gather specific insights. Whether customers are submitting reviews via email or on your website, you control what information they provide and how the form looks.

**What you'll achieve:**
- Match review forms to your brand style and tone
- Collect specific feedback relevant to your products
- Control which fields are required vs optional
- Reduce form abandonment with smart field choices
- Gather richer, more detailed customer reviews

<Info>
**Impact:** Customized review forms collect 30-40% more detailed feedback and see 20% higher completion rates than generic forms
</Info>

## Why Customize Review Form Inputs

Generic review forms miss opportunities to gather valuable, specific feedback:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Generic Form Problems" icon="circle-xmark">
    **What happens with default settings:**

    - Generic placeholder text ("Write your review...")
    - Irrelevant questions for your product type
    - Too many required fields (abandonment)
    - No brand personality
    - Misses product-specific insights

    **Result:** Short, vague reviews like "Great product!"
  </Card>

  <Card title="Customized Form Benefits" icon="circle-check">
    **What happens with customization:**

    - Brand-specific tone and language
    - Targeted questions for your products
    - Optimized required vs optional fields
    - Matches your site design
    - Collects actionable insights

    **Result:** Detailed reviews with specific, helpful information
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
**Example:** A clothing brand asking "How does it fit?" gets 3x more sizing details than asking "What do you think?" ‚Äî and sizing info reduces returns by 15-25%.
</Tip>

## How to Access Review Form Customization

Yuko Dashboard ‚Üí **Reviews ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Review Collection Form**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Form Type" icon="toggle-on">
    Select which review form to customize:

    **Email review forms:**
    - Forms sent in review request emails
    - Customer clicks link, sees form
    - Most common review collection method

    **On-site review forms:**
    - Embedded on product pages
    - "Write a Review" button
    - Always available for customers

    **Both forms can be customized separately** or use the same settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to Form Customization" icon="sliders">
    In the Review Collection Form settings, you'll see customization sections:

    - Rating display
    - Photo uploads
    - Review content fields
    - Reviewer information
    - Thank you message
    - Next product prompts
    - Footer text
    - Advanced styling (CSS)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customize Each Section" icon="pen">
    Work through each section, adjusting:

    - Field labels and placeholder text
    - Required vs optional settings
    - Character limits and validation rules
    - Display order and visual styling
    - Help text and examples

    **Save changes** after each section or at the end.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview Your Form" icon="eye">
    Use the preview function to see how your form looks:

    - Desktop view
    - Mobile view
    - Email vs on-site versions
    - Test form submission

    **Test thoroughly** before going live.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Launch and Monitor" icon="rocket">
    Activate your customized review form and track results:

    - Completion rate (target: 60%+)
    - Average review length (target: 50+ words)
    - Photo upload rate (target: 15-30%)
    - Time to complete (target: under 2 minutes)

    **Adjust based on data** ‚Äî if completion rate drops, you may have too many required fields.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Should I make review text required or optional?" icon="question">
    **Recommended: Make review text optional**

    **Why optional is better:**

    **Completion rate:**
    - Required text: 40-50% completion rate
    - Optional text: 70-80% completion rate
    - 30-40% more reviews with optional text

    **Quality:**
    - Required text often results in low-effort "Great product!" reviews
    - Optional text means those who write WANT to share (better quality)
    - 5-star ratings without text are still valuable social proof

    **Customer experience:**
    - Not everyone has time to write detailed reviews
    - Some customers prefer quick star rating
    - Forcing text creates friction and resentment

    **What to do instead:**
    - Make review text optional
    - Add helpful prompts to encourage detailed responses
    - Offer incentive for longer reviews (50+ words)
    - Show example reviews to guide customers

    **Example approach:**
    - Rating: Required
    - Review text: Optional, with placeholder prompts
    - Photos: Optional, with incentive
    - Bonus: "Write 50+ words for 15% off your next order"

    **Results:**
    - 70-80% leave star rating
    - 40-50% also write text
    - 20-30% upload photos (with incentive)
    - Overall: More total reviews, good text quality from those who engage

    <Tip>
    **Pro tip:** Track your completion rate. If it drops below 60%, you have too many required fields. Remove requirements until completion rate is 70%+.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many custom fields should I add?" icon="sliders">
    **Recommended: 2-3 custom fields maximum**

    **Field count impact on completion rate:**

    **3-5 total fields: 75-80% completion** ‚úÖ Optimal
    - Star rating (required)
    - Review text (optional)
    - Name (required)
    - Email (required)
    - Photos (optional)

    **6-8 total fields: 60-70% completion** ‚ö†Ô∏è Acceptable
    - All basic fields above
    - +1-2 custom questions (dropdowns)
    - Still manageable

    **9-12 total fields: 40-50% completion** ‚ùå Too many
    - Basic fields
    - +4-6 custom questions
    - Feels like a survey
    - High abandonment

    **13+ fields: 20-30% completion** ‚ùå Way too many
    - Customers give up
    - Only most motivated complete
    - Not worth the lost reviews

    **What custom fields to add:**

    **High value (add these):**
    - Fit/sizing (for clothing)
    - Skin type (for beauty)
    - "Would you recommend?" (any product)

    **Medium value (add if relevant):**
    - How long used (supplements, tech)
    - Age range (some products)
    - Use case (versatile products)

    **Low value (skip these):**
    - Occupation (rarely relevant)
    - Detailed demographics (too personal)
    - Multiple rating scales (confusing)

    **Best practice:**
    Start with basic fields only. After 50+ reviews, analyze what information is missing. Add 1-2 custom fields based on actual needs. Monitor completion rate.

    <Warning>
    **Every extra field costs reviews:** Each additional required field reduces completion rate by 5-10%. Only add custom fields if the information is worth losing reviews over.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I have different forms for different product types?" icon="split">
    **Yes ‚Äî and you should for very different product categories**

    **When to use different forms:**

    **Scenario 1: Different product categories**
    - Store sells clothing AND electronics
    - Clothing needs "How does it fit?" field
    - Electronics needs "Ease of setup" field
    - Solution: Create separate forms per category

    **Scenario 2: Different price points**
    - Low-price items (under $20): Quick form (3 fields)
    - High-price items (over $100): Detailed form (5-6 fields)
    - Customers more willing to write detailed reviews for expensive purchases

    **Scenario 3: Different customer segments**
    - B2C customers: Simple, friendly tone
    - B2B customers: Professional, detailed questions
    - Different language and expectations

    **How to set up multiple forms:**

    **In Yuko:**
    1. Create default review form (used for most products)
    2. Create custom forms for specific categories
    3. Assign forms to product categories or tags
    4. System uses category-specific form when available

    **Example setup:**
    - Default form: 5 fields, general questions
    - Clothing form: + "How does it fit?" dropdown
    - Beauty form: + "Skin type" and "Results" fields
    - Food form: + "Taste rating" and "Recipe ideas"

    **When NOT to use different forms:**
    - All products are similar (e.g., only selling shoes)
    - Small store (under 50 products) - not worth complexity
    - Testing phase (start with one form, optimize later)

    <Check>
