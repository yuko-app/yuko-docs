## Get 50% More Reviews with One Gentle Reminder

Most customers who don't respond to the first review request aren't refusing - they're just busy and forgot. A single friendly reminder sent 3-5 days later can increase your review collection rate by 50% (from 5-10% to 7-15%).

**What you'll achieve:**

- Automatically remind non-responders without manual work
- Increase review collection by +50%
- Stay top-of-mind without being annoying
- Collect feedback from customers who needed a nudge

<Info>
  **Impact:** Adds +50% more reviews | **Time to set up:** 5 minutes
</Info>

## How It Works

Yuko Dashboard ‚Üí **Reviews ‚Üí Emails ‚Üí Review Reminder Email**

![Rr2new](/images/rr2new.png)

### The Automatic Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Request Sent" icon="paper-plane">
    Customer receives initial review request 5 days after order delivery (or your set delay).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Yuko Tracks Response" icon="eye">
    Yuko automatically tracks whether customer clicked the review link or submitted a review.

    **Yuko knows who responded** - only non-responders get the reminder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reminder Waits" icon="clock">
    For customers who didn't respond, Yuko waits your set delay (default: 5 days after first email).

    **No duplicate emails** - customers who already reviewed won't get reminded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reminder Sends" icon="bell">
    Non-responders receive a gentle, friendly reminder with the same review link.

    **One reminder only** - Yuko won't send multiple reminders (that would be spam).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customer Reviews" icon="pen-to-square">
    The reminder jogs their memory and they write the review.

    **Additional response rate:** +2-5% of customers respond to reminder.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How much do reminders actually help?" icon="chart-line">
    **Real impact numbers:**

    **Without reminder:**

    - 5-10% of customers leave reviews after first email
    - Rest never review (too busy, forgot, or not interested)

    **With one reminder:**

    - Original 5-10% still respond to first email
    - Additional 2-5% respond to reminder
    - **Total: 7-15% review rate** (+50% improvement)

    **Why it works:**

    - Most non-responders just forgot
    - Reminder jogs their memory
    - Shows you care about feedback
    - Second chance without being pushy

    <Info>
      **Rule of thumb:** Expect reminder to add 30-50% more reviews on top of your base rate. If 10 people reviewed from first email, expect 3-5 more from reminder.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will customers find reminders annoying?" icon="face-frown">
    **If done right, no:**

    **What makes reminders annoying:**

    - ‚ùå Sent too soon (next day)
    - ‚ùå Multiple reminders (2-3 follow-ups)
    - ‚ùå Pushy or demanding tone
    - ‚ùå Guilt trips ("You promised to review")
    - ‚ùå Fake urgency ("Last chance!")

    **What makes reminders acceptable:**

    - ‚úÖ Wait at least 3-5 days
    - ‚úÖ Send only ONE reminder
    - ‚úÖ Friendly, casual tone
    - ‚úÖ Easy unsubscribe
    - ‚úÖ Acknowledge it's optional

    **Industry standard:** One reminder email 3-7 days later is considered normal and acceptable business practice.

    <Tip>
      **Test yourself:** How do you feel when companies send you reminder emails? Copy the ones that feel friendly, avoid the ones that feel pushy.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I offer an incentive in the reminder?" icon="gift">
    **Two approaches:**

    **Option 1: Same incentive as first email** (recommended)

    - If first email offered 10% off for reviewing, reminder mentions same offer
    - Consistent messaging
    - Simple to manage

    **Option 2: Add NEW incentive in reminder**

    - First email: No incentive or small incentive
    - Reminder: Bigger incentive ("Special offer: 15% off if you review now")
    - Can boost reminder response rate
    - Risk: Trains customers to wait for reminder to get better deal

    **Our recommendation:**

    - New stores: Offer same incentive in both emails
    - Established stores: Test adding incentive only in reminder
    - Always: Make sure incentive is for honest feedback, not positive reviews

    <Warning>
      **Don't:** Increase incentive in reminder or customers will learn to ignore first email and wait for the "better offer" in the follow-up.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if customers already reviewed but still get reminder?" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **This shouldn't happen, but if it does:**

    **How Yuko prevents this:**

    - Tracks who clicked review link
    - Tracks who submitted reviews
    - Automatically excludes them from reminder list
    - Only true non-responders get reminded

    **If customer complains they already reviewed:**

    1. Apologize ("Sorry, technical glitch!")
    2. Check Yuko dashboard - did their review actually submit?
    3. If yes: Exclude them from future reminders
    4. If no: Their review may not have gone through - help them resubmit

    **This is rare** - Yuko's tracking is very accurate. But technical glitches can happen (email bounces, spam filters, etc.).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I send reminders to old customers who never reviewed?" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    **Yes, but be careful:**

    **Good candidates for one-time reminder:**

    - Ordered 30-60 days ago
    - Never received review request (you just set up Yuko)
    - Never reviewed

    **Don't send reminder to:**

    - Orders from 90+ days ago (too old, they forgot)
    - Customers who already reviewed
    - Customers who unsubscribed

    **How to do it:**

    1. Go to Yuko Dashboard ‚Üí Customers
    2. Filter: "No review" + "Last 60 days"
    3. Send one-time manual review request
    4. Wait 5 days, send one-time manual reminder

    <Tip>
      **Label it differently:** For old customers, say "We recently added a review system" so they know why they're hearing from you now.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I personalize reminder differently than first email?" icon="sparkle">
    **Yes, small changes help:**

    **First email tone:**

    - Professional and appreciative
    - "We'd love your feedback"
    - Focus on helping other customers

    **Reminder email tone:**

    - More casual and friendly
    - "Just checking in"
    - Acknowledge they're busy
    - Even more brief

    **Example differences:**

    **First email:** "Thank you for your recent purchase! We'd love to hear about your experience with [Product]. Your review helps other customers make confident decisions."

    **Reminder:** "Hi again! Just a quick follow-up - if you have 2 minutes, we'd love your thoughts on [Product]. No worries if you're too busy!"

    **Key difference:** Reminder is shorter, more casual, and acknowledges the follow-up nature.
  </Accordion>
