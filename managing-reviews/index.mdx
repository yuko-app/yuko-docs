### Review Status Types

Every review has a status that determines whether it's visible to customers:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title=" Published">
    **Reviews visible on your store**

    **What this means:**

    - Shown on product pages and review widgets
    - Counted toward average ratings
    - Included in search-indexed content
    - Fully visible to shoppers

    **When to publish:**

    - Helpful, constructive feedback
    - Verified or genuine reviews
    - Clear and appropriate language
    - Reviews that add value to your store

    Publishing quality reviews strengthens trust and increases conversions.

    **Approved reviews are your social proof** - they drive sales
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pending ">
    **Reviews waiting for your approval**

    **What this means:**

    - Not yet visible to customers
    - Do not affect average rating
    - Require a manual action from you
    - Can be published or moved to spam/trash

    **How reviews become pending:**

    - Auto-publish disabled (all reviews pending)
    - Low star ratings (1-3 stars if auto-publish only 4-5)
    - First-time reviewers (trust verification)
    - Flagged by spam filters (needs manual check)
    - Contains certain keywords (custom moderation rules)

    **What to do with pending reviews:**

    - Review content for quality and appropriateness
    - Approve if legitimate
    - Reject if spam or inappropriate
    - Reply before publishing (address concerns)

    **Response time matters** - approve within 24 hours for best results
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Spam">
    **Reviews marked as spam or fake**

    **What this means:**

    - Hidden from all customer-facing areas
    - Not counted in ratings
    - Stored in the Spam folder
    - Can be restored if incorrectly flagged

    **Common spam indicators:**

    - Irrelevant, nonsensical, or promotional content
    - Attempts to post links or advertisements
    - Duplicate submissions
    - Reviews clearly not linked to real customers

    Use the Spam folder to keep harmful or misleading content out of your store.

    **Don't delete immediately** - review spam folder occasionally for false positives
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Trash ">
    **Reviews removed but recoverable**

    **What this means:**

    - Not visible anywhere
    - Stored in the Trash section
    - Can be restored
    - Permanently deleted after 30 days

    **Why move to trash:**

    - Customer requested deletion
    - Duplicate reviews
    - Review for wrong product
    - Outdated information
    - Policy violation

    **Trash vs Spam:**

    - Trash: Legitimate reviews you don't want displayed
    - Spam: Fake/malicious reviews

    **Recovery option:**

    - Trash allows 30-day recovery period
    - Restore if deleted by mistake
    - Permanent after 30 days

    **Best practice:** Use trash for legitimate deletions, spam for fake reviews
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Review Moderation Strategies

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Manual Moderation">
    **Review and approve each review individually**

    **Best for:**

    - New stores (building initial reviews)
    - Luxury brands (quality control)
    - Sensitive products (need careful review)
    - Stores with spam issues

    **Process:**

    1. All reviews start as "Pending"
    2. You review each one daily
    3. Approve quality reviews
    4. Reject spam/inappropriate
    5. Reply before publishing (optional)

    **Pros:**

    - Complete control
    - Catch all spam
    - Quality assurance
    - Opportunity to reply first

    **Cons:**

    - Time-consuming
    - Slower social proof building
    - Daily commitment required

    **Recommended for:** Stores under 50 reviews/month

    ‚Üí [Set up manual moderation](/managing-reviews/review-moderation)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Auto-Publish with Filters">
    **Automatically approve most reviews, manually review some**

    **Best for:**

    - Growing stores (50-500 reviews/month)
    - Established brands
    - Verified purchase focus
    - Balanced approach

    **Process:**

    1. Auto-publish 4-5 star reviews
    2. Auto-publish verified purchases
    3. Pending: 1-3 star reviews (manual check)
    4. Pending: Unverified reviews
    5. Auto-spam detection

    **Settings:**

    - Auto-approve: 4-5 stars + verified purchase
    - Manual review: 1-3 stars OR unverified
    - Auto-spam: Obvious spam patterns

    **Pros:**

    - Faster social proof
    - Less daily work
    - Catch problems (low ratings)
    - Respond to negative reviews before public

    **Cons:**

    - Some spam may get through
    - Less control over every review
    - Need good spam filters

    **Recommended for:** Most stores

    ‚Üí [Configure auto-publish rules](/managing-reviews/auto-publishing-reviews)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Full Auto-Publish">
    **All reviews go live immediately**

    **Best for:**

    - High-volume stores (500+ reviews/month)
    - Trusted customer base
    - Strong spam filters
    - Hands-off approach

    **Process:**

    1. All reviews auto-published instantly
    2. You review periodically (weekly)
    3. Remove spam after the fact
    4. Reply to reviews as time allows

    **Settings:**

    - Auto-publish: All reviews
    - Spam detection: Auto-flag and hide
    - Moderation: Reactive (not proactive)

    **Pros:**

    - Immediate social proof
    - Minimal time investment
    - Max review volume displayed
    - No moderation bottleneck

    **Cons:**

    - Spam appears briefly
    - Negative reviews live immediately
    - Less quality control
    - Reactive vs proactive

    **Recommended for:** Large stores with good spam filters

    **Risk mitigation:**

    - Strong email verification
    - Product blacklist for problematic items
    - Email blacklist for spam domains
    - Daily spam check
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How often should I check for new reviews?" icon="clock">
    **Daily moderation recommended for best results**

    **Minimum:**

    - Check pending reviews daily (once per day)
    - Respond to 1-2 star reviews within 24 hours
    - Weekly spam folder review

    **Recommended:**

    - Check pending reviews twice daily (morning, evening)
    - Respond to all reviews within 24 hours
    - Daily spam folder review

    **Optimal:**

    - Real-time alerts for new reviews (email notifications)
    - Respond within 1-4 hours
    - Continuous moderation

    **Set up workflow:**

    - Morning: Check pending, approve quality reviews, reply to negative
    - Evening: Second check, thank 5-star reviewers
    - Weekly: Review spam folder for false positives

    **Time investment:**

    - Small stores (less than 20 reviews/month): 15 min/day
    - Medium stores (20-100 reviews/month): 30-45 min/day
    - Large stores (100+ reviews/month): 1-2 hours/day or hire VA

    <Tip>
      **Hire help:** Once you're getting 100+ reviews/month, consider hiring a virtual assistant for moderation. Save yourself 1-2 hours daily.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if a review is unfair or inaccurate?" icon="scale-unbalanced">
    **You can reply, but usually shouldn't delete**

    **If review is factually wrong:**

    **Example:** "Product is plastic, description says metal" **Your options:**

    1. Reply correcting error: "Our product is actually stainless steel as described. We'd love to resolve this. Please email support@..."
    2. If customer ordered wrong product variant, explain politely
    3. If genuinely misunderstood, clarify

    **Don't delete** - reply corrects misinformation publicly

    **If review is subjectively negative:**

    **Example:** "I don't like the color" **Your options:**

    1. Reply: "Sorry it wasn't what you expected! We offer free returns within 30 days."
    2. Leave it - legitimate opinion
    3. Use feedback to improve product photos (color accuracy)

    **Don't delete** - subjective opinions are valid

    **If review is unfair/unreasonable:**

    **Example:** "1 star - shipping took 3 days" (when 3-5 days promised) **Your options:**

    1. Reply: "Thanks for your feedback! We delivered within our 3-5 day window. Sorry it felt too long. For faster shipping, we offer 2-day express option."
    2. Kill them with kindness
    3. Other customers see you're reasonable

    **Don't delete** - your reasonable response makes reviewer look unreasonable

    **When you CAN delete:**

    - Review for completely wrong product
    - Contains false claims (defamation)
    - Offensive/profane language
    - Violates review policies

    **When in doubt:**

    - Reply don't delete
    - Professional response wins hearts
    - Future customers see you handle criticism well
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I reply to every review or just negative ones?" icon="reply-all">
    **Reply strategy depends on review volume**

    **Low volume (less than 20 reviews/month):**

    - Reply to ALL reviews
    - Thank 5-star reviewers
    - Address all concerns
    - Shows high engagement
    - Encourages more reviews

    **Medium volume (20-100 reviews/month):**

    - Skip brief 5-star "Great!" reviews (too many)

    - Reply to detailed 5-star reviews
    - Reply to reviews with questions
    - Skip generic positive reviews (unsustainable)

    **What to prioritize:**

    1. **1-2 star reviews** (respond within 24 hours) - damage control
    2. **Reviews with questions** (answer publicly) - helps other shoppers
    3. **Detailed 5-star reviews** (thank customer) - recognition
    4. **4-star reviews** (address concern) - could become 5-star
    5. **3-star reviews** (understand issue) - improvement opportunity

    - 5-star: "Thank you, [Name]! We're thrilled you're happy with [product]. Customers like you make our day! üíô"

  <Accordion title="Can I edit customer reviews after they're submitted?" icon="pen">
    **Yes, but use carefully and ethically**

    **What you CAN edit:**

    - Fix obvious typos ("amzing" ‚Üí "amazing")
    - Remove customer's personal information (phone, address)
    - Remove offensive language (replace with "[removed]")
    - Correct product assignment (review on wrong product)
    - Fix formatting (add line breaks for readability)

    **What you CANNOT edit:**

    - Change review sentiment (negative ‚Üí positive)
    - Remove valid criticism
    - Add fake positive content
    - Alter star rating
    - Change meaning of review

    **Ethical guidelines:**

    - Edit minimally
    - Never change meaning
    - Transparent edits (note if significant)
    - Only edit for clear improvements
    - Get customer permission if major changes

    **When to edit:**

    **Scenario 1: Customer asks** "Customer emails: "Can you remove my phone number from my review?" **Action:** Edit review, remove phone number, note: "Edited to remove personal info"

    **Scenario 2: Profanity** Review: "This f\*\*\_ing product is sh_t" **Action:** Edit to "This [removed] product is [removed]" OR delete entirely (violates policy)

    **Scenario 3: Wrong product** Review on Product A mentions Product B **Action:** Move review to Product B, note: "Review moved to correct product"

    **Scenario 4: Typo affects meaning** Review: "Product is NOT amazing" Customer meant: "Product is NOW amazing" (typo) **Action:** Email customer asking permission to fix typo

    **What to avoid:**

    - Editing reviews to remove criticism
    - Making negative reviews sound positive
    - Changing star ratings
    - Adding content customer didn't write

    **Transparency wins:**

    - If edit is significant, add note: "(Edited by store to [reason])"
    - Builds trust vs hiding edits
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What should I do with really old reviews?" icon="calendar-days">
    **Keep, archive, or delete based on relevance**

    **Keep old reviews if:**

    - Product hasn't changed significantly
    - Reviews still accurate and relevant
    - Historical social proof valuable
    - Review content high quality

    **Archive/hide old reviews if:**

    - Product significantly improved (old reviews don't reflect current quality)
    - Reviews mention old pricing/policies
    - Product redesigned (old version reviews confusing)
    - Very old (5+ years) and product evolved

    **How to handle:**

    **Option 1: Keep with context**

    - Keep old reviews visible
    - Add store note: "This review is from 2019. We've since updated the product with [improvements]."
    - Shows transparency and improvement

    **Option 2: Archive separately**

    - Move old reviews (3+ years) to "Archived Reviews" section
    - Not displayed by default
    - Available if customers want to see
    - Maintains history without confusion

    **Option 3: Delete if truly irrelevant**

    - Product completely different now
    - Old reviews misleading
    - Reviews for discontinued version
    - Delete but export first (historical record)

    **Best practice:**

    - For products 1-3 years old: Keep all reviews
    - For products 3-5 years old: Archive reviews mentioning outdated info
    - For products 5+ years old: Hide/delete if product significantly changed

    **Add new reviews:**

    - If product improved based on old negative feedback
    - Request new reviews from recent customers
    - Build fresh review base
    - Old negative reviews become less prominent

    <Note>
      **Don't mass-delete:** Gradual archiving better than sudden deletion. Keeps SEO value while maintaining relevance.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
