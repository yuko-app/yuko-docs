## Keep Your Review Section Clean and Professional

Review moderation tools help you maintain quality standards while preventing spam, fake reviews, and inappropriate content. Set up automated filters and manual approval workflows that match your brand's needs.

**What you'll achieve:**
- Block spam and fake reviews automatically
- Filter profanity and inappropriate language
- Set up approval workflows for quality control
- Maintain professional review section
- Protect brand reputation

<Info>
**Impact:** Stores with active moderation see 60% less spam and maintain 25% higher trust scores than unmoderated stores
</Info>

## Profanity Filter

Block or censor offensive language automatically:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Replace with Asterisks">
    **Automatically censor profanity**

    **How it works:**
    - Yuko detects profane words in reviews
    - Replaces with asterisks (f*** or ****)
    - Review text published with censorship
    - Customer message unchanged in database

    **Example:**
    - Customer writes: "This f***ing product is amazing!"
    - Displays as: "This **** product is amazing!"

    **Settings:**
    ```
    Profanity filter:
    [‚úì] Replace with asterisks (****)
    [ ] Hide review entirely
    [ ] Hold for manual review
    ```

    **Pros:**
    - Review sentiment preserved
    - Automatic moderation
    - No manual work
    - Still shows enthusiasm

    **Cons:**
    - Obvious censorship visible
    - May look unprofessional
    - Customer didn't consent to edit

    **Best for:** Casual brands, high review volume

    <Tip>
    **Recommended:** Use replacement for mild profanity. For severe language, use "hold for review" instead.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Hide Review Entirely">
    **Block reviews with profanity**

    **How it works:**
    - Yuko detects profane words
    - Review automatically hidden
    - Sent to spam folder
    - Never published publicly

    **Example:**
    - Customer writes: "This s*** doesn't work"
    - Review ‚Üí Auto-hidden, marked as spam
    - Not visible to other customers

    **Settings:**
    ```
    Profanity filter:
    [ ] Replace with asterisks (****)
    [‚úì] Hide review entirely
    [ ] Hold for manual review
    ```

    **Pros:**
    - Professional appearance
    - No offensive language visible
    - Simple solution

    **Cons:**
    - Lose potentially valuable feedback
    - Customer's legitimate complaint hidden
    - May violate review transparency
    - False positives (innocent words flagged)

    **Best for:** Conservative brands, family products

    **Warning:** May hide legitimate negative reviews. Check spam folder weekly for false positives.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Hold for Manual Review">
    **Flag profanity for your review**

    **How it works:**
    - Yuko detects profane words
    - Review sent to pending
    - You review manually
    - Decide: Approve, edit, or reject

    **Example:**
    - Customer writes: "Amazing! Worth every damn penny"
    - Review ‚Üí Pending (contains "damn")
    - You review: Approve (positive context)
    - Or edit: "Amazing! Worth every penny"

    **Settings:**
    ```
    Profanity filter:
    [ ] Replace with asterisks (****)
    [ ] Hide review entirely
    [‚úì] Hold for manual review
    ```

    **Pros:**
    - Context-aware decisions
    - Keep valuable feedback
    - Edit rather than delete
    - Catch severe language

    **Cons:**
    - Manual work required
    - Slower review publishing
    - Time investment

    **Best for:** Most stores (balanced approach)

    **Time investment:** 5-10 min/day if catching 5-10 reviews

    <Check>
    **Best practice:** Hold for manual review. This lets you approve mild language in positive reviews while blocking severe profanity in negative reviews.
    </Check>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Spam Detection

Automatically catch and block spam reviews:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Email Domain Filtering" icon="envelope">
    Block reviews from known spam domains.

    **How it works:**
    - Yuko maintains list of disposable email domains
    - Reviews from these domains auto-flagged as spam
    - Examples: tempmail.com, 10minutemail.com, guerrillamail.com

    **Your email blacklist:**
    - Add specific domains you've seen spam from
    - Block competitor domains (optional)
    - Add patterns (like *@spam.com)

    ‚Üí [Email blacklist setup](/managing-reviews/email-blacklist)

    **Automatic blocking:**
    - Disposable email domains
    - Known spam email patterns
    - Multiple reviews from same email

    **Action:** Reviews auto-hidden or pending for manual verification
  </Step>

  <Step title="Duplicate Detection" icon="clone">
    Catch copy-paste spam reviews.

    **How it works:**
    - Yuko checks if review text matches existing reviews
    - 90%+ similarity = duplicate
    - Flags for manual review

    **Catches:**
    - Exact copy-paste spam
    - Bot-generated reviews (same template)
    - Competitor attack (same negative review on multiple products)

    **Settings:**
    - Similarity threshold (default: 90%)
    - Action: Hold for review or auto-hide
    - Timeframe: Check duplicates in last X days

    **False positives:**
    - Legitimate customers with similar feedback
    - Common phrases ("Great product!")
    - Generic positive reviews

    **Solution:** Manual review of flagged duplicates
  </Step>

  <Step title="Suspicious Patterns" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Detect unusual review submission patterns.

    **What Yuko flags:**
    - Multiple reviews from same IP in short time
    - Reviews submitted in rapid succession
    - New email submitting many reviews quickly
    - Reviews for products not in order history

    **Examples of suspicious activity:**
    - 10 reviews from same IP in 1 hour (bot)
    - Same person reviewing 20 products (hasn't bought)
    - Burst of 1-star reviews in 10 minutes (attack)

    **Action:** Flagged reviews sent to pending for your review

    **Review the pattern:**
    - Legitimate? Approve
    - Spam attack? Bulk reject and blacklist email/IP
    - Competitor sabotage? Report and block
  </Step>

  <Step title="Content Analysis" icon="brain">
    AI detects spam-like content.

    **What Yuko analyzes:**
    - Gibberish text ("asdfghjkl")
    - Too generic ("good product")
    - Contains URLs or links
    - All caps (SCREAMING)
    - Excessive punctuation (!!!!!!)

    **Spam indicators:**
    - Links to other websites
    - Contact information (phone, email)
    - Promotional language ("buy now", "discount code")
    - Off-topic content
    - Review for wrong product entirely

    **Confidence score:**
    - High confidence spam (95%+): Auto-hide
    - Medium confidence (70-95%): Pending review
    - Low confidence (less than 70%): Publish (with other filters)

    **You can adjust sensitivity:**
    - Aggressive: Catch more spam, more false positives
    - Balanced: Default setting
    - Lenient: Fewer false positives, miss some spam
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manual Moderation Workflow

## Review Restrictions & Limits

Prevent abuse with submission limits:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One Review Per Customer Per Product" icon="user">
    **Prevent duplicate reviews**

    **How it works:**
    - Customer can only review each product once
    - Attempting second review shows error
    - Or: Replaces first review with new one

    **Why enable:**
    - Prevents spam (same person, many reviews)
    - Authentic review count
    - No gaming the system

    **Exception:** Customer can update/edit their existing review

    ‚Üí [Review restrictions](/managing-reviews/review-restrictions-limits)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Rate Limiting" icon="clock">
    **Limit reviews per time period**

    **Settings:**
    - Max 5 reviews per email per day
    - Max 10 reviews per IP per day
    - Cooldown: 1 hour between reviews

    **Why enable:**
    - Stops spam bots
    - Prevents bulk spam attacks
    - Normal customers unaffected

    **Action:** Excess reviews auto-flagged or blocked

    ‚Üí [Review restrictions](/managing-reviews/review-restrictions-limits)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Minimum Purchase Requirement" icon="shopping-cart">
    **Only verified purchases can review**

    **How it works:**
    - Customer must have purchased product
    - Order verified via email match
    - Unverified reviews blocked or pending

    **Why enable:**
    - 95% spam reduction
    - Higher quality reviews
    - Trust signal (verified badge)

    **Trade-off:** Guest checkouts can't review easily

    ‚Üí [Auto-publishing settings](/managing-reviews/auto-publishing-reviews)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Email Verification" icon="envelope-circle-check">
    **Require email confirmation**

    **How it works:**
    - Customer submits review
    - Email sent with verification link
    - Click to verify and publish
    - No click = review not published

    **Why enable:**
    - Confirms real email address
    - Prevents fake email spam
    - Higher quality reviews

    **Trade-off:** 20-30% lower completion rate (adds friction)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Check Spam Folder Weekly" defaultOpen icon="calendar-check">
    **Don't let legitimate reviews get lost**

    **Why:**
    - Spam filters have false positives
    - Legitimate reviews accidentally flagged
    - Customer complaint looks like spam
    - Profanity filter caught mild language

    **Weekly spam review:**
    1. Go to Reviews ‚Üí Spam
    2. Sort by date (newest first)
    3. Scan for false positives
    4. Approve legitimate reviews
    5. Delete real spam

    **What to look for:**
    - Detailed negative reviews (not spam, real complaint)
    - Reviews with mild profanity in positive context
    - First-time reviewers flagged incorrectly
    - Product names triggering keyword filters

    **Action:**
    - Approve false positives
    - Update filters to prevent repeat false flags
    - Reply to recovered negative reviews

    **Time:** 10 minutes weekly
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Start Conservative, Loosen Gradually" icon="gauge-simple-high">
    **Begin with strict moderation, relax as you learn patterns**

    **Month 1: Manual moderation**
    - Review every single review
    - Learn your spam patterns
    - Understand review quality
    - Set standards

    **Month 2: Add auto-approve for 5-star verified**
    - 5-star + verified = auto-publish
    - Everything else = manual review
    - See if spam increases
    - Adjust if needed

    **Month 3: Add auto-approve for 4-star verified**
    - 4-5 star + verified = auto-publish
    - 1-3 star OR unverified = manual
    - Faster social proof
    - Still catching problems

    **Month 4+: Fine-tune based on data**
    - Analyze spam rate
    - Check moderation time
    - Optimize rules
    - May loosen further or keep as-is

    **Why this works:**
    - Learn before automating
    - Build confidence in filters
    - Catch issues early
    - Scale moderation gradually

    <Tip>
    **Don't rush automation:** Taking 3 months to fully automate is better than dealing with spam attack from being too permissive too soon.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Monitor Your Spam Rate" icon="chart-line">
    **Track spam to know if filters are working**

    **Key metrics:**
    - Spam rate: % of reviews marked as spam
    - False positive rate: % of spam that should have been approved
    - Manual moderation time: Hours per week
    - Auto-publish rate: % of reviews auto-approved

    **Good spam rate:**
    - less than 5% spam = Excellent (filters working)
    - 5-10% spam = Good (normal level)
    - 10-20% spam = High (tighten filters)
    - 20%+ spam = Crisis (major filter issue)

    **If spam increasing:**
    1. Check spam folder for patterns
    2. Identify common spam sources (emails, IPs)
    3. Add to blacklist
    4. Tighten auto-publish rules
    5. Enable email verification

    **If spam too low (with high false positives):**
    1. Filters may be too strict
    2. Check spam folder for legitimate reviews
    3. Loosen keyword filters
    4. Adjust profanity sensitivity
    5. Review false positive patterns

    **Monthly review:**
    - Export spam reviews to spreadsheet
    - Look for patterns
    - Adjust filters accordingly
    - Track improvement
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use Blacklists Strategically" icon="ban">
    **Block repeat offenders, not entire categories**

    **Email blacklist:**
    - Add specific spam email addresses
    - Block disposable email domains
    - Blacklist competitor domains (optional)

    **Don't blacklist:**
    - Entire email providers (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.)
    - Customer who left one negative review
    - Domains used legitimately

    ‚Üí [Email blacklist guide](/managing-reviews/email-blacklist)

    **Product blacklist:**
    - Temporarily blacklist new products (manual review first 30 days)
    - Blacklist problematic products (quality issues)
    - Remove from blacklist once stable

    **Don't blacklist:**
    - Products permanently (defeats auto-publish)
    - Products just because they get negative reviews
    - All products (use manual moderation instead)

    ‚Üí [Product blacklist guide](/managing-reviews/product-blacklist)

    **Best practice:** Blacklists are for exceptions, not rules. Use sparingly and review quarterly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Respond Before Moderating Negatives" icon="reply">
    **Address concerns privately, then decide on publishing**

    **Workflow for negative reviews:**
    1. Negative review in pending
    2. Read carefully
    3. Contact customer privately via email
    4. Offer solution, apology, replacement
    5. THEN decide: Approve with reply, or resolve privately

    **Example:**
    - Customer: "Product broke after 2 days" (2-star review)
    - You (email): "So sorry! This isn't typical. We'll send replacement immediately + 20% refund. Can we make this right?"
    - Customer: "Yes, thank you!"
    - You: Approve review with public reply: "We're sorry about this issue and have sent a replacement. We stand behind our quality."
    - OR: Customer updates to 5-star after resolution

    **Why this works:**
    - You resolve the issue
    - Customer often updates review or doesn't mind it being public
    - Your public reply shows you care
    - Other customers see you fix problems

    **Don't:**
    - Hide legitimate complaints without addressing
    - Approve negative reviews without responding
    - Only publish positive reviews (looks suspicious)

    <Check>
    **Best practice:** Reply to ALL negative reviews before approving. Show you care publicly. This turns negative reviews into trust builders.
    </Check>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How strict should my profanity filter be?" icon="gauge">
    **Depends on your brand and audience**

    **Strict filtering (hide or censor):**
    - Family-friendly products
    - Children's products
    - Conservative industries
    - Religious/values-based brands
    - B2B products

    **Lenient filtering (hold for review):**
    - Casual lifestyle brands
    - Adult products
    - Streetwear/youth brands
    - Products marketed to adults
    - Authentic brand voice

    **Very lenient (no filter):**
    - Edgy brands
    - Adult-only products
    - Brands where profanity matches voice
    - High trust in customer base

    **Recommended for most stores:**
    - Hold for manual review
    - Approve mild language in positive context
    - Reject severe profanity in negative reviews
    - Context matters

    **Example decisions:**
    - "This is f***ing amazing!" ‚Üí Approve (enthusiasm)
    - "This sh*t doesn't work" ‚Üí Reject (add-spam)
    - "Damn good quality" ‚Üí Approve (mild, positive)
    - Multiple profanity + angry ‚Üí Reject or edit

    <Tip>
    **Balance authenticity with professionalism:** Customers appreciate authentic language, but excessive profanity looks unprofessional to other shoppers.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if spam filters are too aggressive?" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **Loosen filters if catching too many false positives**

    **Signs filters are too strict:**
    - 20%+ of spam folder is legitimate reviews
    - Customers complaining reviews not appearing
    - Many detailed negative reviews marked as spam
    - Profanity filter catching common words

    **How to loosen:**

    **1. Adjust spam sensitivity:**
    - Settings ‚Üí Spam Detection ‚Üí Sensitivity
    - Change from "Aggressive" to "Balanced"
    - Or "Balanced" to "Lenient"

    **2. Review profanity word list:**
    - Check custom words you added
    - Remove overly broad terms
    - Add whitelist exceptions
    - Change action from "hide" to "hold for review"

    **3. Reduce keyword blocking:**
    - Remove competitor names from blacklist (if false positives)
    - Allow certain patterns (like "refund")
    - Check email domain blacklist

    **4. Test with sample reviews:**
    - Submit test reviews with edge cases
    - See if they get through
    - Adjust filters accordingly

    **Balance:**
    - Some false positives are acceptable (weekly spam review catches them)
    - Better to catch too much than too little initially
    - Loosen gradually based on data

    **Monitoring:**
    - Check spam folder weekly
    - Track false positive rate
    - Target: less than 10% false positives
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Should I moderate reviews in other languages?" icon="language">
    **Yes, but may require translation or native speaker**

    **Challenges:**
    - Profanity filters are English-focused
    - Spam patterns differ by language
    - You may not understand the content
    - Context is harder without language fluency

    **Solutions:**

    **Option 1: Manual review all non-English**
    - Set rule: Non-English reviews ‚Üí Pending
    - Use Google Translate to review
    - Approve if seems legitimate
    - Reject obvious spam

    **Option 2: Hire native speaker for moderation**
    - Virtual assistant fluent in language
    - Reviews non-English submissions
    - Provides moderation decision
    - Cost: $5-15/hour depending on language

    **Option 3: Auto-approve with reactive moderation**
    - Allow non-English reviews to publish
    - Customers flag inappropriate content
    - You review flagged reviews with translation
    - Remove if problematic

    **Option 4: Limit to one language**
    - Only accept English reviews
    - Block non-English submissions
    - Simpler moderation
    - But: Loses international customers

    **Recommended:** Manual review non-English with Google Translate. Most international customers leave legitimate reviews, spam is usually obvious.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I handle competitor attacks?" icon="shield">
    **Detect and block coordinated negative review campaigns**

    **Signs of competitor attack:**
    - Sudden spike in 1-star reviews
    - Reviews submitted in short time (minutes/hours)
    - Similar wording across reviews
    - Reviews mention competitor favorably
    - No purchase history
    - Same IP address or email pattern

    **Immediate action:**
    1. Check reviews for patterns
    2. Look at submission timestamps
    3. Check IP addresses (if available)
    4. Verify purchase history
    5. Flag all suspicious reviews

    **Blocking the attack:**
    1. Blacklist email addresses/domains
    2. Blacklist IP range if needed
    3. Enable CAPTCHA for review submission
    4. Require verified purchase temporarily
    5. Report to platform if violates terms

    **Responding:**
    - Don't hide legitimate negative reviews (even if timing is suspicious)
    - DO hide reviews with no purchase history + spam patterns
    - Reply to questionable reviews: "We can't find your order. Please email us at support@ to verify and we'll address your concern."
    - Document attack (screenshots, IP logs) for legal action if needed

    **Prevention:**
    - Verified purchase requirement
    - Email verification
    - Rate limiting (max reviews per IP)
    - CAPTCHA on review forms

    **Long-term:**
    - Build positive review volume (harder to manipulate)
    - Monitor review patterns
    - Respond to all reviews (shows engagement)
    - Report competitor to authorities if ongoing

    <Warning>
    **Don't delete legitimate negative reviews just because of timing.** If the complaints are valid, address them. Only remove obvious fake reviews with no purchase history.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I automate moderation completely?" icon="robot">
    **Mostly yes, but some manual oversight recommended**

    **Fully automated moderation:**
    - Auto-publish based on rules (rating, verified, etc.)
    - Spam filters auto-hide obvious spam
    - Profanity filter auto-censors
    - No daily manual work

    **Works well for:**
    - High-volume stores (1,000+ reviews/month)
    - Established stores with good filters
    - Trusted customer base
    - Limited moderation time/resources

    **Still need periodic manual checks:**
    - Weekly spam folder review (false positives)
    - Monthly filter performance review
    - Quick response to flagged reviews
    - Crisis management (attack, PR issue)

    **Cannot fully automate:**
    - Context-dependent decisions
    - Nuanced negative reviews
    - First-time controversial product reviews
    - Legal/sensitive content

    **Recommended approach:**
    - Automate 90% of moderation
    - Manual oversight 10%
    - Weekly 15-minute review of exceptions
    - Adjust filters quarterly

    **Reality check:**
    - Full automation = 98% accuracy
    - 2% still needs human judgment
    - That 2% is often most important (negative reviews, edge cases)
    - Don't eliminate manual review entirely

    <Note>
    **Sweet spot:** Automate routine approvals, manually review exceptions. This balances efficiency with quality control.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
